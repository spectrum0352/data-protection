# Recon email

Email reconnaissance involves gathering information about email addresses, email infrastructure, and communication patterns associated with individuals or organizations. It is commonly used during security assessments, penetration testing, and threat intelligence activities.

---

# Email Tracking

## Purpose of Email Tracking

Email tracking is used to monitor the delivery and interaction of emails sent to intended recipients.

## Information Gathered Through Email Tracking

Email tracking tools may collect the following information:

* Recipient system IP address
* Estimated geolocation
* Browser and operating system details
* Mail server and email service provider information
* Email delivery notifications
* Email open/read notifications
* Approximate read duration
* Proxy or VPN detection
* Link click tracking and interaction analysis

## Common Email Tracking Tools

*(For authorized security testing and educational purposes only)*

* Mailtrack
* Infoga
* EmailTrackerPro

## Security & Privacy Considerations

Unauthorized use of email tracking for malicious activities may violate privacy laws, organizational policies, and ethical guidelines.

# Email Footprinting

## Purpose of Email Footprinting

Email footprinting is the process of discovering email addresses and related information associated with a target individual or organization.

## Email Footprinting Techniques

### Search Engine Reconnaissance

* Searching publicly available websites and domains
* Using search engines to identify exposed email addresses
* Discovering email patterns used by organizations

### Reconnaissance on Victim-Owned Domains

* Searching contact pages, support portals, and documentation
* Reviewing WHOIS records and public domain registrations
* Enumerating email naming conventions

### Social Engineering Techniques

* Phishing
* Vishing
* Pretexting
* Information elicitation

### Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT)

* Searching social media platforms
* Reviewing online directories and public forums
* Identifying leaked email addresses from public sources

### Search Engine Dorking

Using advanced search operators to discover exposed email addresses or sensitive information on publicly accessible systems.

# Email Enumeration & Analysis

## Email Enumeration

* Identifying valid email accounts
* Verifying email address existence
* Enumerating mail servers and SMTP configurations

## Email Scanning

* Email header analysis
* Mail server identification
* SMTP banner grabbing
* SPF, DKIM, and DMARC validation checks

## Email Vulnerability Analysis

* Misconfigured mail servers
* Open relay identification
* Weak authentication mechanisms
* Email spoofing risks
* Phishing exposure assessment

---

### Reconnaissance Sources

**Open Sources**

* Public websites
* Social media platforms
* Online directories
* Public repositories and forums

**Closed Sources**

* Internal portals
* Restricted-access platforms
* Subscription-based databases

---

**Phishing for Information**

Attackers may use phishing campaigns to gather:

* Employee email addresses
* Internal communication details
* Organizational structure information
* Credentials and sensitive data

Security awareness and email filtering controls are critical to reduce phishing risks.

---

**Key Points**

* Email reconnaissance should only be performed with proper authorization.
* Activities must comply with legal, ethical, and organizational security policies.
* Security teams commonly use these techniques during penetration testing and threat intelligence operations.
* Organizations should implement email security controls, monitoring, and user awareness training to reduce exposure.
